# Microsoft Dataverse for Power Apps – Important Concepts

## 1. What is Dataverse?

Microsoft Dataverse is a secure, cloud-based data platform used to store and manage business data for Power Platform applications.

Interview Answer:

Dataverse is a cloud-based relational database provided by Microsoft that allows us to securely store, manage, and model business data for Power Apps, Power Automate, Power BI, and other Microsoft services.

---

## 2. Why Use Dataverse?

Advantages:

* Secure and scalable
* Supports relational data
* Role-based security
* Business rules support
* Audit history
* Better performance than SharePoint for large applications
* Native integration with Power Platform

---

## 3. Dataverse Components

### Tables

Tables store business data.

Examples:

* Employee Table
* Customer Table
* Request Table

Equivalent in databases:

Table = Database Table

---

### Rows

Rows represent records.

Example:

| Employee ID | Name   | Department |
| ----------- | ------ | ---------- |
| 1001        | Venkat | IT         |

---

### Columns

Columns store attributes.

Examples:

* Name
* Email
* Status
* Department

---

## 4. Types of Columns

### Single Line of Text

Stores short text.

Example:

Employee Name

---

### Multiple Lines of Text

Stores long text.

Example:

Comments

---

### Choice

Stores predefined values.

Example:

Status:

* New
* Approved
* Rejected

---

### Yes/No

Stores Boolean values.

---

### Number

Stores numeric values.

---

### Currency

Stores monetary values.

---

### Date and Time

Stores dates.

---

### Lookup

Creates relationships between tables.

Example:

Employee → Department

---

### File

Stores files.

---

### Image

Stores images.

---

### Autonumber

Automatically generates IDs.

Example:

REQ-0001

---

## 5. Types of Tables

Provided by Microsoft.

Examples:

* Account

---

### Custom Tables

Created by developers.

Example:

Employee Requests

---

### Activity Tables

Track activities.

Examples:

* Emails
* Appointments
* Tasks

---

## 6. Relationships (Very Important)

### One-to-Many (1:N)

One Department can have many Employees.

Department
|
|------ Employee1
|------ Employee2

Example:

One Department → Many Employees

---

### Many-to-One (N:1)

Many Employees belong to one Department.

---

### Many-to-Many (N:N)

One Student can enroll in many Courses.

One Course can have many Students.

---

Interview Question:

Q: What types of relationships are available in Dataverse?

Answer:
Dataverse supports One-to-Many, Many-to-One, and Many-to-Many relationships.

---

## 7. Primary Key

Every table contains a unique identifier.

Example:

EmployeeId

Used internally by Dataverse.

---

## 8. Alternate Key

Used to uniquely identify records using business values.

Example:

Employee Number

Benefits:

* Prevent duplicates
* Support integrations

Interview Question:

Q: What is an Alternate Key?

Answer:
Alternate Key is a business-specific unique identifier used to uniquely identify records apart from the primary key.

---

## 9. Business Rules

Business Rules enforce validations without code.

Example:

If Salary > 100000
Set Approval Required = Yes

Benefits:

* No coding required
* Reusable
* Server-side validation

Interview Question:

Q: What are Business Rules?

Answer:
Business Rules are no-code validation mechanisms used to enforce business logic on Dataverse tables.

---

## 10. Calculated Columns

Values are automatically calculated.

Example:

Total Amount = Quantity × Price

---

## 11. Rollup Columns

Aggregate data from related tables.

Example:

Total Orders of Customer

---

## 12. Security Roles (Very Important)

Dataverse provides role-based security.

Privileges:

* Create
* Read
* Write
* Delete
* Append
* Append To
* Assign
* Share

Access Levels:

### User

Only own records.

### Business Unit

Records within business unit.

### Parent Child Business Unit

### Organization

Entire organization.

Interview Question:

Q: What are Security Roles?

Answer:
Security Roles control user access to Dataverse tables and records based on privileges and access levels.

---

## 13. Business Units

Business Units help organize users and security.

Example:

Organization
├── HR BU
├── Finance BU
└── IT BU

---

## 14. Auditing

Tracks record changes.

Example:

Who changed:

* Status
* Amount
* Owner

Interview Question:

Q: What is auditing in Dataverse?

Answer:
Auditing tracks data changes and records who modified the data and when.

---

## 18. Dataverse vs SharePoint

| Feature        | Dataverse       | SharePoint          |
| -------------- | --------------- | ------------------- |
| Purpose        | Relational data | Document management |
| Relationships  | Strong          | Limited             |
| Security       | Advanced        | Basic               |
| Performance    | High            | Medium              |
| Large Data     | Excellent       | Limited             |
| Business Rules | Available       | Not available       |
| Audit          | Built-in        | Limited             |

---

## 19. Integration with Power Apps

Common Functions:

Create Record:

```PowerFx
Patch(
    Employees,
    Defaults(Employees),
    {
        Name:"Venkat"
    }
)
```

Update Record:

```PowerFx
Patch(
    Employees,
    Gallery1.Selected,
    {
        Status:"Approved"
    }
)
```

Retrieve Records:

```PowerFx
Filter(Employees, Status="Active")
```
